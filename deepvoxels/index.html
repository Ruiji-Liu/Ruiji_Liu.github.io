DeepVoxels: Learning Persistent 3D Feature Embeddings

Teaching 3D to 2D generative models.

Vincent Sitzmann, Justus Thies, Felix Heide, Matthias Niessner, Gordon Wetzstein, Michael Zollhöfer

Paper Supplemental Material

2D Generative Models don't Understand 3D

Deep Generative Models today allow us to perform highly-realistic image synthesis in a variety of settings. While each generated image is of high quality, a major challenge is to generate a series of coherent views of the same scene. This would require the network to have a latent space representation that fundamentally understands the 3D layout of the scene; e.g., how would the same chair look from a different viewpoint?

Unfortunately, this proves challenging for existing generative neural network architectures that are based on a series of 2D convolution kernels. Instead of actually parameterizing 3D transformations, they will instead explain training data in a higher-dimensional feature space, leading to poor generalization to novel views at test time - such as the output of Pix2Pix trained on images of the cube above.

With DeepVoxels, we introduce a 3D-structured neural scene representation for view synthesis. DeepVoxels encodes the view-dependent appearance of a 3D scene without having to explicitly model its geometry. At its core, DeepVoxels is based on a Cartesian 3D grid of persistent embedded features that learn to make use of the underlying 3D scene structure. Our approach combines insights from 3D geometric computer vision with recent advances in learning image-to-image mappings based on adversarial loss functions. DeepVoxels is supervised, without requiring a 3D reconstruction of the scene, using a 2D re-rendering loss and enforces perspective and multi-view geometry in a principled manner.
