Bio

Hi! I am Ruiji Liu, a Graduate Research Assistant at Kantor Lab in CMU Robotics Institute. I am currently working on research about field robotics autonomous navigation within crop fields with Prof. George Kantor. I am interested in research problems related to perception and control for navigate or operate various robotic systems (UGV, UAV, & legged robot) autonomously in diverse environments. My research goal is to enable robots to replace humans in dangerous tasks like rescue or discovery in unknown environment, as well as in tedious tasks such as transporting goods. Specifically, I am interested in research areas including CV, SLAM, path planning, & control with machine learning (DL & RL) methods applied. Previously, I received my master of science degree in mechanical engineering with concentration in robotics and control system at CMU. My master's thesis is "LiDAR-Based Crop Row Detection Algorithm for Over-Canopy Autonomous Navigation in Agriculture Fields". I received my bachelor of science degree in mehcanical engineering at Penn State University.
